About K10s

K10s is not just another Kubernetes management platform; it‚Äôs the outcome of a university project initiated at Karel de Grote University of Applied Sciences in Antwerp, Belgium. Originally conceived as a tool to visually represent Kubernetes clusters, K10s quickly evolved into a powerful open-source solution that goes beyond basic cluster management.

This platform offers a hierarchical view of Kubernetes ecosystems, including superclusters, pools, nodes, and pods. With K10s, users can better understand the performance, interconnections, and management of their Kubernetes resources, all in one place. Beyond its functionality, K10s reflects the innovation and collaborative spirit of Karel de Grote University, where it all began.

On this site, you'll not only find detailed information about the software, but also the journey behind K10s, including presentations made to pitch the idea to professors, promo videos, quotes, photos, examples, and much more.

How to Contribute

We welcome contributions to the K10s project! To ensure a smooth and efficient process, please follow these guidelines:

  • Familiarize yourself with the project's goals and structure.
  • Fork the repository and create a new branch for your changes.
  • Provide detailed descriptions of your changes in your pull request.
  • Ensure your code is well-tested and doesn't introduce regressions.
  • Reference issue numbers in your pull request description, if applicable.

Security

Reporting Vulnerabilities

Security is a top priority. If you discover any vulnerabilities, please follow these steps:

  • Notify us immediately via a private communication channel.
  • Do not publicly disclose the vulnerability until we have had time to address it.
  • Allow us time to investigate and provide updates on the resolution process.

Your efforts to help keep K10s secure are greatly appreciated. Thank you for your contribution!
